Competetive Audit
Learing Apps
Question Kahoot! Typing.com Codecombat
Engagement: How engaging is the app? Is it visually appealing? Does it have sound and animation to capture the user's attention? Does it have a good balance of challenge and reward? Kahoot is engaging for the user from the start, with distinct colours used for variety of options, it seems like there is something for everyone. The learning experience itself is engaging and fun. Trying to beat other students is followed with sound effects and animations, getting the correct answer feels rewarding. The interface is visually appealing in its simplicity, but at the same time engaging. It doesnt need to be flashy and colorful to capture users attention, and in my opinion the overall feel of the website is calm and nice. Using the clouds animation to capture this. The visual appeal of the game reminds me of early 2000s flash games, this is not necessarily mean its bad, just outdated. The experience itself is engaging, with different animations and sounds to make the interaction more enjoyable. The challange was well balanced with the rewards which I got.
Progress tracking: How well does the app track the user's progress? Does it provide feedback and recommendations to help users improve their performance? Does it use data to personalize the learning experience? User progress in challenges is tracked well, showing the total points at the end of every round. With hotstreaks to hype up your progress with sound and animations effects as bonus. It doesnt give you hints and you have to count on your knowledge or pure luck, this can lead to having next to nothing points with no help from the app to keep up with others. This can lead to frustration and sabotage from the user. At the start of each lesson, there is a short video talking about the upcoming challange. The challanges itself uses animations and sound design to emphasize the user input. At the end of the challange there is star rating and accuracy. This makes the user to try have the most starts, learning more. The progress is tracked on visually presented map, it always points you to the next task. The in game console always gives you hints, what lines of code you can write and not write. This changes accroding on your gameplay, for example when you do moveRight multiple times the game tells you can do moveRight(3) to repeat that 3 times.
User experience: How user-friendly is the app? Is it easy to navigate and use? Does it have clear instructions and prompts? Does it have a well-designed interface? I would say that the user experience is good and easy to understand. Creating challanges and inviting others is easy to do. The app it self is ok to navigate in, sometimes it can get a bit confusing, but nothing to make the user leave. Overall the app is well designed, but not perfect. This app is easy to use, the experience is fun and rewarding. When I first opened the app I exactly knew where to click to start lessons. The interface itself is appealing and engaging to use, making me want to go trough all the lessons and challanges. When I first opened the app, it was clear where to go to start the game. I think the instructions and overall design is well made and easy to understand for the user.
